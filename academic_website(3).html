About Me

[PHOTO]

I am a Distinguished Professor of Hematology and Medical Oncology at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, where I lead cutting-edge research in blood cancer genomics and computational biology.

My research focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying hematologic malignancies through advanced sequencing technologies and computational approaches. I specialize in single-cell multi-omics analysis, including scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, scCITE-seq, and single-cell DNA sequencing.

With expertise in both wet lab experimentation and bioinformatics, I bridge the gap between experimental biology and computational analysis. My work combines mathematical modeling, statistical analysis, and software development to uncover novel insights into cancer biology.

As a mathematician and programmer, I develop innovative computational tools and algorithms for next-generation sequencing data analysis, with the goal of translating these findings into clinical applications.
